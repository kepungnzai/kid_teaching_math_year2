🧪 Ratios: Mixing Cleaning Solution! 🧹

What is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities. It tells us how much of one thing we need compared to another!

Today, we're mixing a cleaning solution. The instructions say: mix 2 parts bleach with 13 parts water. Let's figure out how much of each we need!

🧴 Bleach : Water = 2 : 13  |  We need 60 L total — how much bleach? 🤔

Each part = Total Volume ÷ Total Parts = 60 L ÷ 15 = 4 L per part
4
Bleach needed = 2 parts × 4 L = 8 L of bleach 🧴
5
Water needed = 13 parts × 4 L = 52 L of water 💧

🎯 The Answer:

We need 8 L of bleach and 52 L of water!
Ratio 2:13 → 8 L bleach + 52 L water = 60 L total cleaning solution 🧹✨

🧠 The Ratio Method

1 Add the ratio parts together to find total parts: 2 + 13 = 15
2 Divide total volume by total parts: 60 ÷ 15 = 4 L per part
3 Multiply each ratio part by the value per part!
💡 Bleach: 2 × 4 = 8 L  |  Water: 13 × 4 = 52 L
