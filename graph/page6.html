📏 How Far Did They Go? 🦅🐦🦃

Distance vs Time — A New Way to See Speed!

Remember how the Peregrine Falcon zoomed up fast and dropped fast? And the Ostrich ran at a constant speed? In Adventure 4 we saw their speed. But now we ask: How far did each bird travel? 🏃

The distance a bird travels depends on its speed. A bird that goes fast for longer covers more ground. Let's see who goes the farthest after 3 seconds and 5 seconds!

🧠 Distance = Speed × Time. Fast bird + more time = more distance! 📏

🦜 Quick Recap: The Three Birds

🦅
Peregrine Falcon

Speeds up very fast (0→90 km/h in 2s) then slows down fast. It covers a lot of distance quickly!

🐦
Pigeon

Speeds up and down gently (0→30→0). It doesn't travel very far in a short time.

🦃
Ostrich (Running)

Speeds up steadily (0→50 km/h at a constant rate). Keeps going farther and farther!

🧠 What We Learned About Distance

1 🦅 Falcon at 3s: 49m — It zoomed ahead fast, but then slowed down, so it only gained 21m more in the next 2 seconds.
2 🦃 Ostrich at 3s: 12m — It started slowly, but kept speeding up, gaining 23m in the next 2 seconds!
3 🔍 Compare the lines: The Falcon's line curves and flattens (it stops gaining distance fast). The Ostrich's line curves upward (it keeps going faster)!
💡 Distance = Speed × Time! If you go fast, you cover more ground. But if you keep speeding up, you cover even more!
