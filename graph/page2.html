📈 Let's Explore Line Graphs!

What's a Line Graph?

A line graph is like a bar graph's cousin! Instead of bars, we use dots connected by a line. This helps us see how things change over time — going up, going down, or staying the same! 📈📉

Line graphs are perfect for showing trends — like "Is it getting hotter each day?" or "Are my video game scores improving?"

🧠 Line graphs show change over time — follow the line to see the story! 👀

🔍 Parts of a Line Graph

📏
Y-Axis (Height)

The vertical line. Shows the amount — like scores, votes, or temperature!

↔️
X-Axis (Bottom)

The horizontal line. Shows the categories or time — like animals, days, or levels!

⏺️
Data Points (Dots)

Each dot shows the value at one point. Higher dot = bigger number!

〰️
The Line

The line connects the dots. Going up 📈 = increasing! Going down 📉 = decreasing!

🔄 Bar Graph vs Line Graph

📊
Bar Graphs

Best for comparing separate groups side by side. Like comparing favorite pets!

📈
Line Graphs

Best for showing change over time or in a sequence. Like scores going up and down!

🧠 How to Read a Line Graph

1 Read the title — what story is this graph telling?
2 Look at the bottom (x-axis) — is it showing time, levels, or categories?
3 Look at the side (y-axis) — what numbers are we measuring?
4 Follow the line — does it go up 📈 (increase) or down 📉 (decrease)?
5 Steep line = fast change! Flat line = little change!
