🎂 More Than One Whole! 🧮

What happens when fractions are bigger than 1?

So far you've learned about fractions like ½ and ¼ — which are less than 1. But what happens when the top number is bigger than the bottom?

Example: 3 ÷ 2 or 3/2. That's more than one whole! Let's share some cakes! 🎂

🤔 3 cakes shared between 2 people — how much does each person get?
3 ÷ 2 = ?

Press play to find out! 🎬

👋 Ready! Click play to share the cakes!
3 − 2 = 1 (remainder)
So 3 ÷ 2 =
1½ (One and a Half!)
🎂 Fraction
3/2
🍰 Mixed
1½
🔢 Decimal
1.5

🎉 3/2, 1½, and 1.5 all mean the same thing!

🧠 Let's Review

1Started with 3 whole cakes. 🎂🎂🎂
2Gave 1 whole cake to each person. That's 2 cakes given. 🎂🎂
33 − 2 = 1 — 1 cake leftover! This is the remainder! 🎂
4Cut the leftover cake into 2 halves = ½ each! ✂️
5Each person gets 1 + ½ = 1½ cakes! 🎉

🔑 Key Concepts

🎂

Improper Fraction

Numerator bigger than denominator, like 3/2. More than 1 whole!

🍰

Mixed Number

Whole numbers + fraction, like 1½ (one and a half).

🔢

Decimal

3/2 = 1.5. 1 whole + 5 tenths!

➖

The Remainder

3 − 2 = 1. The remainder gets cut into denominator pieces!
