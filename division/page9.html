🧁 Finding the Mean (Average)! 📊

What is the Mean?

The mean (also called the average) is a way to find a fair share for everyone! 🚀

If we have different amounts of something, the mean tells us how much each person would get if we shared equally. ✨

To find the mean: Add up all the numbers, then divide by how many numbers there are! 🎉

🧁 Cupcake Class Party!

📚 Three students brought cupcakes for a class party:

👧 Lily brought 3 cupcakes  |  👦 Max brought 4 cupcakes  |  👧 Ava brought 2 cupcakes

How many cupcakes would each student get if they shared equally?

👧
Lily
🧁 🧁 🧁
3
👦
Max
🧁 🧁 🧁 🧁
4
👧
Ava
🧁 🧁
2
📌 Step 1: Add up all cupcakes: 3 + 4 + 2 = 9 🧁
📌 Step 2: Divide by number of students: 9 ÷ 3 = 3
🧁 The mean is 3 cupcakes per student!
3
✨ If they share equally, each student gets 3 cupcakes!
(3 + 4 + 2) ÷ 3 = 9 ÷ 3 = 3

📚 Example 2: Books Read This Month

📖

Books Read by 4 Friends

Four friends read different numbers of books this month. What is the mean number of books read?

Tom: 5 📖 Sarah: 3 📖 Jake: 7 📖 Emma: 5 📖
Step 1: Add: 5 + 3 + 7 + 5 = 20 📖
Step 2: Divide: 20 ÷ 4 = 5
📊 Mean = 5 books per friend

The mean is 5 books — if they shared equally, each friend read 5 books! 🎉

🍎 Example 3: Fruit Picking

🍎

Apples Picked by 5 Children

Five children went apple picking. What is the mean number of apples picked?

Sam: 6 🍎 Zoe: 4 🍎 Ben: 8 🍎 Ella: 5 🍎 Noah: 7 🍎
Step 1: Add: 6 + 4 + 8 + 5 + 7 = 30 🍎
Step 2: Divide: 30 ÷ 5 = 6
📊 Mean = 6 apples per child

The mean is 6 apples — if they shared equally, each child gets 6 apples! 🎉

🧠 Remember: How to Find the Mean

2 Count how many numbers there are.
3 Divide the total by the count. That's the mean! 🎉
